MAS Monetary Policy Statement at a Glance

What did MAS decide?

MAS will increase slightly the rate of appreciation of the S$NEER policy band

The visual shows a slightly steeper appreciation path, while the width and centre of the policy band remain unchanged.
What is happening in the economy?

Higher imported energy costs are colliding with a moderation in growth momentum

External shock

Shipping through the Strait of Hormuz has been severely constrained since late February, and global prices of crude oil, natural gas, and related compounds have risen sharply. MAS says this is weakening growth among major trading partners and pushing imported costs higher.

Domestic activity

Singapore’s economy expanded 4.6% year on year in Q1 2026, supported by manufacturing and services tied to the global AI capex cycle. On a quarter-on-quarter seasonally adjusted basis, GDP fell 0.3%, reflecting moderation after strong growth late last year.

How does MAS see the outlook?

Growth is likely to slow, while inflation is expected to pick up and remain elevated over the next few quarters

Inflation outlook for 2026

1.5–2.5%
MAS raised both MAS Core Inflation and CPI-All Items inflation forecasts from 1.0–2.0% to 1.5–2.5%.

Growth outlook for 2026

2.0–4.0%
MAS said 2026 growth is likely to step down from the above-trend pace recorded in 2025, with an updated GDP forecast to be provided in May.
What are the risks?

Risks to inflation and growth remain elevated

Persistent energy disruption

A more persistent disruption to energy supplies will exacerbate inflationary pressures worldwide and deepen the drag on growth.

Intermediate input shortages

Shortages of key intermediate inputs could abruptly curtail industrial production and hit externally oriented sectors.

Tighter global financial conditions

A further tightening in global financial conditions could compound downside risks to growth.

Pullback in AI-related investment

An unexpected pullback in AI-related investment could weaken one of the main supports to Singapore’s current growth resilience.
