MAS Monetary Policy Statement at a Glance

What did MAS decide?

MAS will let the Singapore dollar strengthen a little faster

Main move

MAS slightly increased the rate at which the Singapore dollar policy band is appreciating. In plain language, that means MAS is allowing the Singapore dollar to rise a bit faster over time.

What did not change

MAS did not change the width of the policy band and did not shift the level at which the band is centred. So this was a measured adjustment rather than a full reset of the policy framework.

What is happening in the economy?

Energy prices are up, while growth is still solid but losing some speed

Prices coming in from abroad

Shipping disruption through the Strait of Hormuz has pushed up the prices of oil, gas, and related inputs. That means Singapore is paying more for some imports, and those higher costs can eventually feed into household bills and business expenses.

Activity at home

Singapore’s economy still grew at a firm year-on-year pace in the first quarter of 2026, helped by manufacturing and services linked to global AI investment. But output slipped slightly from the previous quarter, which suggests momentum is cooling after a strong run.

How does MAS see the outlook?

MAS expects slower growth this year and somewhat higher inflation

Inflation in 2026

1.5–2.5%
MAS raised its forecasts for both core inflation and overall inflation to this range, up from 1.0–2.0% previously.

GDP growth in 2026

2.0–4.0%
MAS said growth this year is likely to step down from the above-trend pace recorded in 2025, with an updated forecast to follow in May.
What are the risks?

The main risks are a longer energy shock and a sharper slowdown in global demand

Energy prices stay high

If disruptions to energy supplies last longer, inflation could rise further and growth could weaken more.

Factories face shortages

If key intermediate inputs become harder to get, industrial production could be cut back abruptly.

Financial conditions tighten

If global borrowing conditions become tougher, households and businesses may spend less.

AI investment cools

If global AI-related investment slows unexpectedly, one of Singapore’s current growth supports could weaken.
